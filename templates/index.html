How to Interpret Sentiment Scores

The sentiment score ranges from -1 (most negative) to +1 (most positive).

  • Above 0.05: Positive Sentiment (Good)
  • Between -0.05 and 0.05: Neutral Sentiment
  • Below -0.05: Negative Sentiment (Bad)

Examples:

  • +0.8 — Strong Positive: “Apple stock skyrockets on record profits!”
  • +0.2 — Mildly Positive: “Tesla announces new model release.”
  • 0.0 — Neutral: “Microsoft holds annual developer conference.”
  • -0.2 — Mildly Negative: “Amazon faces minor delivery issues.”
  • -0.7 — Strong Negative: “NVIDIA hit with major antitrust lawsuit.”

Note: Sentiment scores reflect the tone of news headlines, not guaranteed stock performance.
